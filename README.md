## Framework 2.0 is the sixth volume in a cumulative theoretical series (Framework 1.0–2.0) that models the cell as a self-renewing active hydrogel system operating under a Basal Glycolytic Regime (BGR) / OXPHOS dual-flux energetic architecture. This volume adds a third defining dimension — relationship to the extracellular matrix — to the energetic and immune dimensions established in earlier volumes, and tests the resulting three-dimensional model of cellular state directly against experimental data.

## Ten chapters, organized in two halves:

Chapters 1–5 — Architecture of the organism. The three-dimensional definition of cellular state; dormancy as a transit state rather than a fixed cell type; senescence as branch closure within a differentiation tree; a formal semi-Markov stochastic model of state balance; and the collapse of three proposed dormancy "locks" into a single shared-node network (p27, NAD⁺/NADH, acetyl-CoA).

Chapters 6–9 — Malignancy as defeat/co-option of that architecture. Debris littering as a third physical mechanism of immune evasion; why surgery and combined chemo-immunotherapy fail to eliminate all residual clones; a full catalog of factors governing clonal dormancy; and a structural comparison of growth/stability programs in normal versus oncogenic clones.

Chapter 10 — Final synthesis. Separates six potentially novel framework contributions from established literature, and proposes three cross-cutting experimental checks capable of testing the monograph's core claims simultaneously.
